# REPORT-ON-WALK-POWERED-WIRELESS-CHARGING


## Overview
This project demonstrates a walk-powered wireless charging system that harvests kinetic energy generated during walking using piezoelectric materials. The harvested energy is converted into electrical power, stored, and transmitted wirelessly to charge low-power electronic devices. The system promotes sustainable energy usage and explores the integration of renewable energy harvesting with wearable technology.

---

## Objective
- Convert mechanical energy from walking into electrical energy  
- Utilize piezoelectric sensors for energy harvesting  
- Enable wireless charging of small electronic devices  
- Reduce dependency on conventional power sources  
- Promote eco-friendly and sustainable energy solutions  

---

## Working Principle
- Piezoelectric materials embedded in a shoe insole generate AC voltage when subjected to pressure during walking.
- The generated AC voltage is converted into DC using a bridge rectifier.
- The rectified output is stored in a rechargeable battery.
- A voltage regulator ensures a stable output suitable for charging.
- The regulated power is transmitted wirelessly to charge compatible devices.

---

## Components Used
- Flexible piezoelectric materials (films or ceramics)  
- Bridge rectifier  
- Rechargeable battery (Li-ion / NiMH)  
- Voltage regulator  
- Wireless charging module  
- Wires and connectors  
- Shoe insole or wearable base  
- Multimeter and soldering tools  

---

## Methodology Summary
- Select flexible and durable piezoelectric materials.
- Design an insole that allows maximum compression during walking.
- Place multiple piezo elements in a grid pattern to improve energy harvesting.
- Rectify and store the generated electrical energy.
- Regulate voltage and enable wireless power transmission.
- Test output voltage, charging efficiency, and user comfort.

---

## Expected Outcomes
- Sustainable energy generation from walking motion  
- On-the-go wireless charging  
- Integration into wearable technology  
- Reduced electronic waste and battery dependency  
- Potential use in public spaces with high foot traffic  

---

## Advantages
- Renewable and eco-friendly energy source  
- Scalable for wearable and infrastructure applications  
- Educational value in energy harvesting and power electronics  
- Supports innovation in wireless charging systems  

---

## Limitations
- Low energy output per step  
- High cost of piezoelectric materials  
- Durability concerns under repeated mechanical stress  
- Limited suitability for high-power devices  

---

## Applications
- Wearable electronics  
- Fitness trackers and low-power gadgets  
- Smart footwear  
- Public energy-harvesting infrastructure  

---

## Conclusion
The walk-powered wireless charging system highlights the feasibility of harvesting energy from everyday human motion. While challenges such as low output power remain, the project demonstrates a promising step toward sustainable and wearable energy solutions.
